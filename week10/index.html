4. Discussion: Cycles (Blender) vs Customizable Renderer (WebGPU)

Advantages of Cycles in Blender:

  • Very user-friendly: easy scene setup, lighting with HDRIs, and material creation using Principled BSDF.
  • Production-ready path tracer with excellent global illumination, caustics, and physically based materials out of the box.
  • Fast preview in Rendered Viewport and many built-in features (shadow catcher, denoising, GPU acceleration).
  • Huge library of assets, textures, and community support.

Disadvantages of Cycles / What WebGPU enables that Cycles lacks:

  • Cycles is a "black box" , limited ability to modify core rendering algorithms or implement custom shading effects from scratch.
  • In WebGPU, we have full control over the ray tracing pipeline, shaders, and acceleration structures, allowing experimentation with new techniques (e.g., custom importance sampling, neural rendering).
  • WebGPU runs in the browser portable, no installation needed, real-time interaction possible.

Features in Cycles that would be challenging to implement in WebGPU:

  • Robust denoising (OptiX or OpenImageDenoise integration).
  • Full spectral rendering and complex caustics handling.
  • Complex volume rendering and subsurface scattering.
  • Production-level path guiding and many ight sampling optimizations.

Overall, Cycles is excellent for quick, high-quality production rendering, while WebGPU offers educational value and full algorithmic freedom at the cost of development time.
